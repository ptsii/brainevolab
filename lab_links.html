Links of interest:

 

Templeton Foundation: grant funding from the Templeton has made many of our recent research initiatives possible.

Stone Age Institute, an institute (of which Dr. Schoenemann is a research scientist, and with which the lab is closely affiliated) devoted to the study of technology, adaptation, and evolution in human prehistory

 

From the Big Bang to the World Wide Web, an absolutely beautiful educational website covering "Big History," or the history of everything.

 

ScIU: Conversations in Science @ Indiana University: A great general science blog for science happening at IU, which lab members Lana Ruck and Chloe Holden write for and contribute to.

 

The Orangutan Center at the Indianapolis Zoo, where our lab is pursuing collaborative projects on ape cognition with Dr. Rob Shumaker and Dr. Chris Martin.

 

The Ape Cognition and Conservation Initiative, with whom the lab is pursuing collaborative projects on ape cognition with Dr. Jared Taglialatela.

 

Open Research Scan Archive, a collection of over 5,000 high-resolution research scans of crania and postcraina, freely-available to researchers

 

Neurosynth: "a platform for large-scale, automated synthesis of functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) data extracted from published articles"

 

TalkOrigins Archive: TalkOrigins is a newsgroup devoted to the discussion and debate of biological and physical origins. The archive is a collection of articles and essays that provide mainstream scientific responses to the many frequently asked questions (FAQs) that appear in the talk.origins newsgroup and the frequently rebutted assertions of those advocating intelligent design or other creationist ideas.
